Guidance

How to improve your content

From:
Government Digital Service
Published:
20 April 2018

Review and improve content to make sure it stays accurate, current and relevant.

Contents

  1. Planning your improvements
  2. Making improvements
  3. Getting your improvements reviewed

Effective content management means regularly reviewing and improving content.

How-to content (guidance content that tells users how to do something) sould be reviewed on a regular basis.

Start by doing a content review to understand what needs to be updated or improved. Or what content needs to be withdrawn or unpublished.

After you’ve reviewed your content, you can start improving the pages that need work.

Planning your improvements

Improve content in groups

Split content marked for improvement into manageable chunks instead of doing it all at once.

Improving content in groups means you can:

  • iterate your improvement plan as you go along
  • improve high priority content first (content with the most impact on the greatest number of users)
  • review groups of content related to one another
  • choose to do a review and improvement together
  • improve content alongside your day to day content responsibilities

Prioritise your improvements

There are different ways to prioritise what content to improve. Choose the method that works best for your organisation and your users.

Highest priority

Improve your content according to highest priority - content that has the greatest impact for the largest number of users.

This could include:

  • topics or journeys of highest importance to users
  • content with highest traffic volumes
  • broken links or journeys into a service

Topic

Improve content according to topics that your content is tagged to, paying attention to topics that are most important to your users.

Policy area

Improve content according to the policy area your content pages are tagged to, paying attention to the policy areas that are most important to your users.

Plan your approach and set a deadline

The time it takes to improve content varies.

Set an estimated timeframe in which to complete improvements. To do this you could:

  • introduce a team rota to divide content improvement work
  • delegate improvements to specific team members
  • dedicate specific days of the week or month to work on content improvements

Start engagement as early as possible

Engage with your content sponsors or subject matter experts (SMEs) as early as possible to discuss your plan to improve a particular section.

Once you’ve improved the content, share the changes with your content sponsor or SME.

Making improvements

Consider the following factors when you're making improvements.

Withdraw

Start by withdrawing pages that no longer meet a user need or can't be acted on anymore.

When you withdraw a page you should:

  • add a change notes explaining what you've done and why
  • include a link to the latest version of the content in the change notes, if relevant
  • check related pages and inbound links
  • check document collections - withdrawn pages don't appear in collections so you could end up with empty collections or collections with only 1 document in them

Unpublish

Unpublish pages that duplicate content elsewhere on GOV.UK.

When you unpublish a page you should:

  • check if any of the content on different pages meets the same user need and needs to be merged into a single page
  • redirect the URL of the page you're unpublishing to a new page
  • check related pages and inbound links

Work on format, title, summary, and detail

Check the format is correct then improve the title, summary, and detail. Use our guidance to help make improvements.

  • The content must be in the correct format indicating what the page is for.
  • The title must be clear, descriptive and follow GOV.UK style.
  • The summary needs to include the information about what and who the page is for.
  • The detail should give more information about the page, but not repeat what’s already been said in the title and summary.

    • Look at whole user journeys

    Check how your improvements may affect other pages in the user’s journey.

    Content related to a page you’re amending may also need to be reviewed and changed too.

    Get your improvements reviewed

    2i review

    You should get an experienced member of your content team to review your improvements using the 2i checklist.

    Fact check

    In most cases, you won't be making factual changes to your content and won't need a formal fact check.

    But, you might want to get your content sponsor involved in improving your content. For example, you could invite them to a pair writing session.

    Track your improvements

    Keep a document to track which pages you’ve improved.

    Hold regular meetings to stay up to date with progress.

    This will help you review and adapt your improvement objectives and decide what to work on next.

    Share performance results of your content improvements once they’ve been live for a few months.

    This will help content sponsors and the wider team see the benefit of improvements.
